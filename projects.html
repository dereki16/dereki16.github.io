UNCONTAINED VR (Unity C#)


Overview

In this immersive post-apocalyptic world, see if you have what it takes to survive. Uncontained features room unlocking, barricade building, zombie shooting fun.

This is a VR game I developed in Unity with help from Hannah Dale as the level designer / art director, Andrew Aguas as the audio editor, and free assets.

Features

  • Object Pooling to create and store multiple game objects.
  • Navigation and pathfinding so zombies can chase and attack player.
  • XR plug-in framework to help support VR.
  • Randomization of goals to help with replayability.
  • Interactive tutorial to help teach player how they can engage with the world.
  • Input system in options menu allowing for switching between different styles of orientation and turning speed for preferrential movement.
  • UI for win case, lose case, main and pause menu.

VATS (Unity C#)

Overview

Our goal was to create a virtual tank where all sorts of sea life can be viewed with ease.

Virtual Aquarium Tank System or VATS was my Capstone project for college - oversaw by professor Drew Clinkenbeard, that I along with Isaac Torres and Lewis Truong completed. We were in talks with the Monterey Bay Aquarium to have this simulation be featured; however, communication fell through due to COVID.

Responsibilities

  • Create the Fish Evaluation Vector (FEV) meant to provide in depth knowledge on sea life.
  • This was accomplished through the use of JSON files.
  • Provide responsive UI that swaps through the different models and information.
  • Lay out a list view that allows for an easier browse through different sea life.
  • Acquire models and undertake a fair amount of research on each of the sea creatures.
  • Add post processing effects to elicit a deeper blue color for the look of the ocean.

Fragmented (Unity C#)

Overview

Fragmented is meant to be an endless runner mobile game where the player is hurtling through space in a minefield of colorful debris. Think Picasso meets endless space runner matched with a trap beat for music.

Developed by me. Assets provided by Hannah Dale.

Features

  • Object pooling to instantiate and store obstacles.
  • Color randomization per obstacle as well as a complimentary secondary color programmed to be similar to the first in terms of color wheel.
  • Camera shake triggered by impact of obstacles.
  • Four sets of speed for obstacles to travel at depending on boost.
  • Custom sound effects for boost collection and obstacle collision.

Controls

  • Click and hold left mouse button down, then drag to move.
  • Pause button at top right.

*Note: To stop music press outside of game box or go to Options.*

O-PONG (Unity C#)

Overview

O-Pong was the first original game I completed. I had a great deal of love and respect for the original. Its simple, yet challenging and fun. I was inspired to take a unique spin on the idea by making the playing field ovular.

I also made it possible to rotate your paddle to position the ball in a difficult to reach spot, and teleport from one end to another.

Features

  • The manipulation of splines for players' paddles to follow an ovular pattern.
  • Randomized position for ball to spawn in, as well as direction to follow.
  • Options menu allowing player to change both player's and ball's speed.
  • Custom sound effects for player hitting ball and ball going out of bounds.

Controls

  • P1: WS to move up and down. AD to rotate paddles.
  • P2: Arrow keys to do the same.
  • Spacebar to pause.

*Note: If game doesn't load properly, please try refreshing. Only playable on PC.*
